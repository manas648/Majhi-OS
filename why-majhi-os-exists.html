Origin Story

Why Majhi OS Exists.

68% of VP searches stall past week 10. Not because recruiters aren't working. Because no one can see inside the system while it's happening. We built Majhi OS to fix that.

Book a Mission Walkthrough → See the Architecture →

The Problem

Hiring systems fail in silence.

Most recruiting software was built to help teams send outreach, track candidates, and schedule interviews. None of it was built to tell you that a mandate is failing before it collapses.

By the time a VP search stalls — week 10, week 14, week 18 — the failure was visible at week 3. Wrong intake. Wrong channels. Wrong recruiter load. The signal was there. The system couldn't surface it.

The Operational Visibility Gap

Hiring managers don't know a mandate is failing until it's already failed. There's no real-time signal, no health score, no early warning system. That's the gap Majhi OS was built to close.

The Compounding Failure Loop

Bad intake drives poor dossiers. Poor dossiers produce weak outreach. Weak outreach collapses the pipeline. Collapsed pipelines trigger manual recovery. Manual recovery introduces error. The loop compounds.

The Recovery Problem

When a search stalls, most firms escalate to a senior recruiter, restart outreach, or replace the vendor. None of that is fast. Majhi OS was built to detect failure early and execute recovery automatically — before escalation is required.

The Answer

The operating system for hiring — not another recruiting tool.

Majhi OS is not an ATS. Not a sourcing tool. Not a CRM. It's the operational intelligence and execution layer that sits above all of them — detecting mandate failure, executing recovery sequences, and delivering real-time visibility into every active search.

Think: Datadog for hiring systems. The infrastructure that prevents failure and recovers it when it occurs.

Layer 1 — Observability

Real-time mandate health monitoring. Recruiter load, funnel degradation, response decay, hiring velocity, SLA breaches — surfaced continuously, not in weekly reports.

Layer 2 — Intelligence

Operational reasoning across every mandate. Why searches fail, which recruiter patterns work, which recovery actions succeed. The system learns and compounds.

Layer 3 — Autonomous Execution

Recovery sequences, recruiter reassignment, workflow escalation — executed by the system without manual orchestration. The OS acts before you have to.

Layer 4 — Attribution & ROI

Executive dashboards tracking cost saved, hiring velocity, recruiter efficiency, and recovered revenue. CFO and CEO-level operational accountability.
