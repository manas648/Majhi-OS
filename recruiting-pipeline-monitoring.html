The Visibility Gap

Why your current recruiting analytics leave mandates blind.

Most recruiting analytics tools are built on data warehouse architecture — they collect data from your ATS, process it overnight, and surface reports in the morning. This works well for strategic planning and capacity modeling. It fails completely for operational intervention.

A mandate that collapses in week 11 was failing in week 4. The outreach reply rate was down 60%. The pipeline had three viable candidates left. The hiring manager's last feedback was 12 days ago. Monitoring without recovery is just a dashboard. Majhi OS does both.

FAQ

Questions about recruiting pipeline monitoring and Majhi OS.

Is Majhi OS a business intelligence tool?

No. Business intelligence tools (Tableau, Looker) are built for retrospective analysis. Majhi OS is operational infrastructure — it monitors live mandate signals and executes recovery in real time. Think Datadog, not Power BI.

Can I see all my mandates on one dashboard?

Yes. The Hiring Health Score gives you a live operational view of every active mandate — health status, key signals, velocity metrics, and recovery actions in progress — on a single command-center view.

How is this different from setting up alerts in my ATS?

ATS alerts notify you when something has already happened: a candidate hasn't been contacted in X days, a stage has been open for Y days. Majhi OS predicts what is about to happen and executes recovery before you need to intervene. The difference is intervention timing: after the fact vs. before collapse.

Who sees the monitoring data?

Your recruiting team sees the operational signals via the Hiring Health Score dashboard. Leadership gets the executive visibility layer: velocity, cost savings, recovered mandates, recruiter efficiency — translated into strategic metrics, not operational detail.
