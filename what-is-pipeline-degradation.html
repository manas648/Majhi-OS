What Is Pipeline Degradation?

Pipeline degradation is how most searches fail. It does not happen suddenly — it accumulates across weeks of small signals that individually look explainable and collectively produce a stalled mandate.

See Majhi OS in Action →

The Definition of Pipeline Degradation

Pipeline degradation is the progressive weakening of a candidate pipeline in an active search mandate — measured as slowing stage velocity, declining candidate volume, rising stage drop-off rates, and falling response rates on new outreach. Left undetected, pipeline degradation produces a stalled mandate; left unaddressed after detection, it produces a failed one.

The critical characteristic of pipeline degradation is that it is gradual. Unlike a sudden candidate dropout or a hiring manager who explicitly withdraws the mandate, degradation looks like a series of individually explicable events: one slow week of outreach, one shortlist that did not land, one interview cycle that took longer than expected. Only at the pattern level is it recognisable as degradation.

The Four Causes of Pipeline Degradation

Outreach Pool Exhaustion

The recruiter has reached the reachable segment of the target candidate pool. Response rates are falling not because messaging is weak, but because the addressable pool has been contacted and did not respond.

Brief Misalignment

The search is sourcing the wrong candidates. Shortlist approval is low, and new sourcing produces more of the same. The pipeline cannot fill because it is being fed the wrong input.

Decision Bottlenecks

Hiring manager response time is creating candidate dropout between stages. Candidates who wait 2 weeks for interview feedback are either off the market or no longer interested.

Recruiter Capacity Constraints

A recruiter with 5+ active mandates cannot give a degrading pipeline the attention it needs. The degradation accelerates because the recruiter cannot respond to the signals fast enough.

"Pipeline degradation is the mechanism by which 68% of searches stall past week 10. It does not announce itself. It shows up as a slightly slower week, a slightly lower response rate, a slightly longer interview cycle. The infrastructure's job is to recognise the pattern before the recruiting manager does."
