Common Questions

What is candidate pipeline management?

Candidate pipeline management is the process of building, monitoring, and advancing qualified candidates through the stages of a hiring process. For executive searches, effective pipeline management requires real-time health monitoring — not just candidate tracking — because each stage failure is exponentially more expensive than in volume hiring.

How do you fix a broken candidate pipeline?

Fixing a broken pipeline requires diagnosing where it is breaking — outreach conversion, screening quality, HM review latency, or offer-stage candidate loss. Each requires a different intervention. Majhi OS diagnoses the specific failure point and executes the appropriate recovery playbook automatically.

Why do executive search pipelines collapse?

Executive search pipelines collapse for four main reasons: outreach stops converting (response decay), the brief drifts from the actual role requirements, hiring managers slow their review creating candidate loss, or the target talent pool is exhausted with the current strategy. Majhi OS monitors all four in real time.

What is a pipeline recovery playbook?

A pipeline recovery playbook is a structured sequence of actions Majhi OS executes when a mandate pipeline falls below health thresholds: sourcing expansion, outreach pivot, brief recalibration, or hiring manager escalation — depending on the diagnosed root cause. Recovery Playbooks compound over time as the system learns what works.
