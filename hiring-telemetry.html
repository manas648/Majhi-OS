What Is Hiring Telemetry?

Hiring telemetry is the continuous measurement and transmission of operational signals from your recruiting system — response rates, funnel velocity, recruiter utilization, pipeline decay rates, and SLA breach thresholds. It is the data layer that makes real-time hiring observability possible. Without telemetry, you are managing a live hiring system with after-the-fact reporting.

The Six Telemetry Signals Majhi OS Monitors

Majhi OS instruments six categories of hiring telemetry across every active mandate:

📉

Response Decay Rate

Measures the rate at which outreach response rates decline over time — a leading indicator of pipeline collapse before it occurs.

⚡

Funnel Velocity

Tracks how fast candidates move from sourcing to shortlist to offer — identifying where pipelines are slowing or stalling.

👥

Recruiter Load Index

Monitors whether recruiters are operating within healthy thresholds or trending toward overload that degrades mandate quality.

🏥

Mandate Health Score

A composite operational score for each active search — the primary telemetry metric that makes system health visible at a glance.

⏱️

SLA Breach Proximity

Tracks how close each mandate is to breaching its time-to-fill SLA, triggering escalation sequences before breach occurs.

🔄

Recovery Signal Strength

Measures whether recovery actions are working — whether the intervention improved the mandate trajectory or a different sequence is needed.

Telemetry vs. Reporting: A Critical Distinction

Weekly recruiting reports show you what happened last week. Hiring telemetry shows you what is happening right now — and what will happen next week if nothing changes.

“Reporting tells you the mandate stalled. Telemetry tells you it is about to — three weeks before it does.” — Manas Majhi, Founder, Majhi OS

Frequently Asked Questions

What is hiring telemetry?

Hiring telemetry is the continuous measurement of operational signals from your recruiting system — response rates, funnel velocity, recruiter load, pipeline health. It is the data foundation that makes real-time hiring observability possible, as opposed to after-the-fact reporting.

How is hiring telemetry different from ATS reporting?

ATS reports show historical data — what happened last week or last month. Hiring telemetry shows live operational data — what is happening in each mandate right now and what trajectory it is on.

What signals does Majhi OS collect as hiring telemetry?

Response decay rates, funnel velocity, recruiter load index, mandate health scores, SLA breach proximity, and recovery signal strength. These six categories provide full operational visibility across every active search.

Why do executive searches fail without telemetry?

Without telemetry, problems compound silently. A response rate declines from 14% to 6% over three weeks but nobody notices until the pipeline is empty. Telemetry surfaces that decay signal at week one, triggering an intervention before the mandate reaches critical state.

How does hiring telemetry compound over time?

Every telemetry signal Majhi OS collects becomes training data for its failure prediction models. Over hundreds of mandates, the system learns which patterns precede failure — building a proprietary operational intelligence graph.
