What Is the Search Intelligence Engine?

The Search Intelligence Engine is the analytical core of Majhi OS — the system that processes telemetry signals from active mandates and generates operational insights, failure predictions, and recovery recommendations. It transforms raw recruiting data into actionable operational intelligence.

What the Engine Analyzes

The Search Intelligence Engine continuously analyzes six categories of search data:

📊

Mandate Health Trajectory

Tracks whether each active search is improving or degrading — and at what rate — across all operational dimensions.

🔮

Failure Probability Scoring

Assigns a real-time failure probability to each mandate based on current telemetry patterns and historical outcomes.

🎯

Recovery Path Selection

Identifies the highest-probability recovery sequence for each specific mandate state — operationally specific, not generic.

⏱️

Timeline Compression Analysis

Identifies which workflow changes will compress time-to-close without sacrificing candidate quality or offer acceptance probability.

👥

Recruiter Effectiveness Mapping

Maps which recruiter behaviors correlate with success for specific search types, propagating patterns across the team.

🌐

Market Intelligence Integration

Incorporates real-time talent market signals to adjust search strategy when market conditions shift.

The Compounding Intelligence Advantage

The Search Intelligence Engine gets more accurate with every mandate that runs through it. Early in a client engagement, it operates on general patterns. After 10 mandates, it has client-specific intelligence.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Search Intelligence Engine?

The Search Intelligence Engine is the analytical core of Majhi OS — the system that processes telemetry from active mandates and generates operational insights, failure predictions, and recovery recommendations.

How does the engine predict mandate failure?

By analyzing telemetry patterns — response decay rates, funnel velocity changes, recruiter load trends, pipeline thinning — and comparing them to historical failure signatures from previous mandates.

Does the Search Intelligence Engine use AI?

Yes, but specifically operational AI trained on mandate outcomes, not general language models. The engine is domain-specific, trained on executive search telemetry data.

What output does the engine produce?

Clear operational recommendations: which mandates need intervention, which recovery sequences have the highest probability of success, which recruiter behaviors need to change, and what timeline changes will compress time-to-close.

How long before the engine learns a client's specific hiring context?

Typically 3–5 mandates for initial pattern recognition, 10–15 mandates for reliable client-specific intelligence, and 30+ mandates for a comprehensive operational map of the client's hiring system.
