Majhi OS/System Architecture
Architecture

The architecture behind Majhi OS.

Four layers. Each one deeper than the last. Together they form the operational nervous system that prevents hiring failure — and recovers it when it occurs.

Book a Mission Walkthrough → Multi-Agent Systems →

Layer 1

Observability — see inside every mandate in real time.

The first layer is visibility. You cannot fix what you cannot see. Majhi OS continuously monitors every active mandate — funnel velocity, outreach response rates, recruiter load, candidate engagement, SLA proximity — and surfaces the signal before it becomes a symptom.

Think Datadog for your hiring system. Not a dashboard you check on Fridays. A continuous monitoring layer that watches every mandate simultaneously.

Mandate Health Score

A composite real-time score for every active search. Combines pipeline depth, outreach response rate, time-in-stage, and recruiter capacity into a single operational health signal.

SLA Monitoring

Mandate response SLO, recruiter load threshold, pipeline health SLO, close velocity SLO — all monitored continuously. Breach alerts fire before deadlines are missed.

Funnel Visibility

Stage-by-stage pipeline view across every active mandate simultaneously. Bottlenecks appear as they form, not after they've caused a delay.

Layer 2

Intelligence — understand why mandates fail.

The second layer is reasoning. Observability tells you what's happening. Intelligence tells you why — and what to do about it.

Majhi OS builds an operational intelligence graph across every mandate it runs. Over time it learns: which mandate types stall at which stage, which recovery actions work for which conditions, which recruiter behaviors correlate with shortlist quality. This is the layer that compounds.

Failure Pattern Recognition

The system recognizes early failure signatures — specific combinations of low response rate, stage stagnation, and recruiter overload — and classifies the mandate risk before it materializes.

Recovery Matching

When a mandate shows stall signals, the Intelligence layer matches current conditions to historical recovery patterns and surfaces the highest-confidence action sequence.

Compounding Learning

Every mandate adds to the intelligence graph. The system gets smarter with each search — more precise failure detection, more accurate recovery matching, better outcome prediction.

Layer 3

Autonomous Execution — act without waiting.

The third layer is action. When the Intelligence layer identifies a recovery action, the Execution layer runs it — without waiting for a human to log in, read a report, and decide to act.

Recovery sequences, outreach pivots, pipeline rebalancing, hiring manager escalations — all executed by the system, with full audit trail, the moment the condition is detected.

Recovery Playbooks

Pre-learned, continuously refined action sequences. The system selects and executes the right playbook for each failure signature — adjusted outreach, re-engagement sequences, channel pivots, or capacity rebalancing.

Workflow Triggers

Condition-based triggers fire automatically: mandate health drops below threshold → recovery sequence initiates. Reply rate decays → outreach strategy adjusts. Recruiter overloaded → mandate flagged for reassignment.

Full Audit Trail

Every autonomous action is logged with the triggering condition, the playbook selected, the outcome measured. Complete operational accountability — nothing runs in a black box.

Layer 4

Attribution & ROI — make the invisible visible to leadership.

The fourth layer translates operational outcomes into executive language. Cost saved, hiring velocity, recruiter efficiency, recovered revenue — all measured, attributed, and reported.

This is what moves Majhi OS from a recruiting tool into CEO and CFO-level strategic software. The layer that makes the operating system worth the conversation at the board level.

Hiring Velocity Dashboard

Time-to-fill trending across all mandates. Where velocity improved, why it improved, and what the operational action was that caused the improvement.

Recruiter Efficiency

Output per recruiter, shortlist approval rates, response rate trends. Operational accountability that identifies high-performers and surfaces capacity constraints before they cause mandate failure.

Revenue Impact

Unfilled VP and C-suite seats have a measurable revenue cost. Majhi OS quantifies the cost of mandate failure and the value of recovery — making the ROI conversation with the CFO concrete.
