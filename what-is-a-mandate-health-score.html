What Is a Mandate Health Score?

The Mandate Health Score is the unit-level view of hiring system health. Where the Hiring Health Score covers the portfolio, the Mandate Health Score covers the individual search — updated in real time as signals change.

The Definition of a Mandate Health Score

A Mandate Health Score is a real-time composite operational metric calculated for a single active search mandate — aggregating the key performance signals for that specific search into a single number that indicates its current health and the probability of on-time close. It is the unit-level equivalent of the portfolio-level Hiring Health Score, providing granular visibility into the state of each individual mandate rather than the aggregate state of the recruiting function.

The Mandate Health Score updates continuously as new signals come in: a response rate change, a hiring manager delay in reviewing a shortlist, a pipeline stage stall, a recruiter load increase. The score is not a weekly snapshot — it is a live operational instrument.

The Signals That Feed the Score

Outreach Performance

Current response rate vs. mandate baseline, sequence engagement, and pool exhaustion indicators. The earliest and most sensitive component.

Pipeline Velocity

Days per stage vs. SLO target. Slowing velocity is the most reliable mid-stage failure signal — detectable before pipeline thinning becomes visible.

Candidate Volume

Active candidates in pipeline by stage. Thinning at any stage signals sourcing exhaustion or excessive rejection rates.

Stakeholder Engagement

Hiring manager response times and shortlist approval rates. Low engagement is the most common invisible bottleneck in executive searches.

SLO Adherence

Current progress vs. the target close date. Compresses as the SLO approaches without an offer pending — adding urgency weight to the other signals.

Recruiter Load Context

The assigned recruiter's current mandate portfolio, weighted by complexity. High load reduces the score because it predicts future execution risk even if current indicators look healthy.

"The Mandate Health Score does not tell recruiters what they already know. It tells them what they do not yet know but soon will: that the mandate is degrading in week 3, not week 10. The score is valuable precisely because it leads the failure, not follows it."

Score Bands and Actions

75–100: HealthyNo action required; monitoring continues
50–74: DegradingAlert to recruiting manager; early review
25–49: At riskRecovery playbook triggered automatically
Below 25: CriticalEscalation + reassignment initiated
