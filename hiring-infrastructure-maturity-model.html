What Is the Hiring Infrastructure Maturity Model?

The Hiring Infrastructure Maturity Model defines five levels of operational sophistication in executive search infrastructure — from fully manual operations to fully autonomous hiring systems. It borrows the maturity model concept from software engineering (CMMI, DevOps maturity) and applies it to the operational architecture of recruiting systems.

The Five Maturity Levels

The model defines five levels of hiring infrastructure maturity:

1️⃣

Level 1 — Manual

Spreadsheets, email chains, status meetings. No systematic visibility. Problems detected after failure. 68% of VP searches stall at this level.

2️⃣

Level 2 — Tracked

ATS in use, basic pipeline reporting, weekly status reviews. Visibility is historical. Problems detected 2–4 weeks after emergence.

3️⃣

Level 3 — Monitored

Real-time mandate health scores, recruiter load tracking, SLA dashboards. Problems detected within 1 week of emergence.

4️⃣

Level 4 — Predictive

Failure prediction active, telemetry-driven alerts, recovery sequences available. Problems detected before they become failures — at week 3–4.

5️⃣

Level 5 — Autonomous

Full autonomous execution: recovery sequences activate without manual trigger, recruiter rebalancing happens automatically, continuous optimization.

Where Most Organizations Are Today

Most executive search firms and in-house TA teams operate at Level 1 or Level 2. They have an ATS (Level 2 indicator) but no real-time visibility, no failure prediction, and no recovery architecture.

“Level 2 organizations think they have infrastructure because they have an ATS. They do not. They have a database. Infrastructure is what monitors the system running the database.” — Manas Majhi

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Hiring Infrastructure Maturity Model?

The Hiring Infrastructure Maturity Model defines five levels of operational sophistication in executive search — from manual hiring (Level 1) to fully autonomous operations (Level 5). It is a diagnostic framework for assessing where a hiring system is and what it needs to advance.

What level is my organization at?

Most organizations with an ATS are at Level 2. Organizations with real-time dashboards but no failure prediction are at Level 3. Organizations with telemetry-driven alerts and recovery sequences are at Level 4.

How quickly can Majhi OS advance a Level 1 organization?

From Level 1 to Level 4 typically takes 60–90 days. The first 30 days covers instrumentation and baseline telemetry. Days 30–60 activate failure prediction. Days 60–90 deploy recovery sequences and establish autonomous execution for Tier 1 operational tasks.

What does Level 5 autonomy look like in practice?

At Level 5, the hiring system detects a stalling mandate, evaluates recovery options, selects the highest-probability intervention, and executes it — without any human triggering the process.

Is Level 5 necessary or is Level 4 sufficient?

Level 4 (Predictive) delivers the majority of Majhi OS value. Most clients operating at Level 4 see 50-day closes, 82% shortlist approval, and full mandate recovery visibility.
