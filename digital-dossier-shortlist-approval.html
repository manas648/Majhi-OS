The Approval Gap

Why 62% of shortlisted candidates get rejected.

Hiring managers reject shortlisted candidates for two reasons: the candidate doesn’t fit the role, or they don’t have enough information to feel confident advancing them. Most rejections are the second reason.

A hiring manager who receives a resume and a recruiter summary has to fill in the gaps themselves — from the interview, from references, from intuition. When the gaps are too large, they default to rejection. A Digital Dossier closes the gaps before the hiring manager has to ask.

The Evidence Connection

What the dossier provides that drives approval.

The five elements of a Digital Dossier that most directly predict approval are: validated professional history (eliminates ‘I’m not sure about that role they listed’), compensation intelligence (eliminates ‘we might be out of their range’), leadership pattern analysis (answers ‘can they manage the team they’d inherit’), explicit risk flags (addresses ‘I noticed a short tenure’ before the hiring manager does), and brief alignment score (confirms ‘this candidate matches what we agreed to look for’).

Every rejection of a qualified candidate is a cost. It extends the search. It burns recruiter capacity. It delays the hire. The Digital Dossier is the most direct lever Majhi OS has to reduce rejection of qualified candidates.

The Mechanics

How the approval rate improvement works in practice.

The shift from 38% to 82% approval does not happen because Majhi OS sources better candidates. It happens because Majhi OS presents candidates in a format that enables confident decisions — not just introductions.

Before Dossier (38% approval)

Hiring manager receives resume. Reviews during a 45-minute interview. Forms impression. Has questions they couldn’t fully explore. Hesitates. Rejects. Asks for ‘someone stronger.’

After Dossier (82% approval)

Hiring manager receives Digital Dossier 24 hours before the interview. Arrives with specific evidence-based questions. Confirms intelligence from the dossier in person. Makes a confident decision.
