Common Questions

What should ATS reporting include?

ATS reporting should include pipeline stage conversion rates, source effectiveness, time-in-stage metrics, and candidate disposition data. What it typically cannot include — because it was not designed to — is real-time mandate health, failure prediction, and autonomous recovery execution.

Why is ATS analytics not enough for executive search?

ATS analytics is built for volume hiring: tracking hundreds of applicants through a defined process. Executive search does not work that way. Each mandate is a unique operation requiring real-time health monitoring, proactive intervention, and dynamic outreach strategy. Majhi OS was built for this.

Can Majhi OS work with my existing ATS?

Yes. Majhi OS operates above your existing ATS — reading signals from your current stack and acting on them. Your ATS remains your system of record. Majhi OS adds the observability and autonomous execution layer your ATS was never designed to provide.

What is the operational visibility gap in recruiting?

The operational visibility gap is the difference between what your ATS reports and what is actually happening in your searches. An ATS might show 45 candidates in pipeline — but 40 of them have been in HM Review for 3 weeks. Majhi OS surfaces these operational realities in real time.
