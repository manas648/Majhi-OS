What Is Mandate Health?

Mandate health is the real-time operational state of an active executive search. It is not binary (open/closed). It is a continuous signal that tells you, at any moment, whether a search is on track, drifting, at risk, or in active failure. The Mandate Health Score is the primary metric in Majhi OS — the number that drives every other decision in the system.

The Six Failure Modes: How Mandates Collapse

1. Outreach Decay

Reply rates drop below threshold, sourcing activity slows, and no one notices because the mandate is still technically open. Detection window: weeks 3–6. Recovery window: still available. Undetected: mandate becomes ghost pipeline.

2. Shortlist Rejection Loop

Repeated rejections without calibrated feedback. The recruiter keeps submitting against the original brief, which no longer matches the hiring manager's evolved expectations. Recovery requires a rebuilt intake brief.

3. Recruiter Overload

Too many active mandates — low-priority mandates receive zero activity for days or weeks. Detection: mandate-level activity monitoring. Recovery: rebalance load before the mandate is abandoned.

4. Hiring Manager Disengagement

The hiring manager stops responding to submissions and scheduling requests. The mandate does not officially stall — it just stops moving. Detection: engagement scoring. Recovery: escalation protocol.

5. Pipeline Collapse

Active candidates disengage simultaneously because the process ran too long, a competing offer appeared, or the candidate experience degraded. Recovery requires pipeline restart with adjusted process speed.

6. Scope Drift Without Brief Update

The role evolves mid-search — seniority level, reporting structure, compensation band — but the search brief is not updated. Candidates are evaluated against a role that no longer exists. Recovery: rebuild brief and re-screen active pipeline.

The 68% Stall Rate as a System Failure

When 68% of VP searches stall past week 10, the failure is not individual recruiter performance. It is the absence of mandate health monitoring. The signal was available — reply rates were dropping, hiring manager engagement was declining, pipeline velocity was slowing — but no system was watching. By week 10, what was recoverable in week 5 is a failed mandate. The Mandate Health Index shifts the detection window from week 10 to week 4.

Methodology: Mandate health component weights, alert thresholds, and recovery rates reflect Majhi OS operational frameworks developed through direct hiring system engagements through June 2026. Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Mandate Health Score?

A real-time composite score for every active executive search, tracking outreach reply rate, pipeline velocity, shortlist approval rate, recruiter load, hiring manager engagement, and audit trail completeness. It moves before a mandate stalls.

What are the most common reasons executive mandates stall?

Six failure modes account for the majority: outreach decay, shortlist rejection loops, recruiter overload, hiring manager disengagement, pipeline collapse, and scope drift without a brief update. Each is detectable before it becomes terminal.

What is the recovery rate for stalled executive searches?

With mandate health monitoring, recovery rates range from 58% (pipeline collapse) to 85% (recruiter overload). Without monitoring, those same failure modes have 20–50% recovery rates because they are detected too late.

How early can hiring system failure be detected?

Most failure modes are detectable in weeks 2–6. The 68% stall rate at week 10 reflects searches where no monitoring system caught the early signals. Majhi OS detection thresholds are calibrated to trigger in the recovery window, not after it closes.

What does audit trail completeness measure?

The percentage of mandate-level actions — outreach sent, candidate status changed, decisions made — that are captured and attributed. Industry baseline is under 15%; 100% coverage means every action is recoverable for analysis, handoff, or failure diagnosis.
