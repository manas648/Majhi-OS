How We Measure Hiring Health

Most recruiting teams can tell you how many roles are open. Very few can tell you which ones are actually healthy. The Hiring Health Score changes that.

Book a Mission Walkthrough

The core problem with traditional recruiting dashboards is that they report on activity, not health. They show you how many candidates were sourced, how many interviews were scheduled, how many offers were sent. What they don't show you is whether the mandate is actually moving toward a successful close — or silently drifting toward failure.

The Hiring Health Score is Majhi OS's answer to that problem. It synthesizes 12 operational signals across every active mandate into a single number, updated in real time, that tells a recruiting leader exactly which searches are on track, which are degrading, and which require immediate intervention.

The 12 Signals We Track

Each signal is weighted based on its predictive correlation with mandate failure. The weights are adjusted over time as the system learns from outcomes across mandates.

Response Decay Rate
Rate at which outreach response rates are declining over the past 14 days
Pipeline Velocity
Time between pipeline stages vs. benchmark. Slowdowns compound.
Recruiter Load Index
Active mandates per recruiter relative to historical capacity threshold
Shortlist Conversion
% of sourced candidates reaching shortlist. Below 38% signals intake misalignment.
Hiring Manager Engagement
Response time and feedback quality from the hiring manager
Outreach Saturation
% of ICP candidates already contacted in prior rounds
Days on Mandate
Current age vs. predicted close date. Divergence triggers early warning.
Offer Acceptance Trajectory
Whether the current pipeline has offer-ready candidates within 15 days
Candidate Drop-Off Rate
% of candidates disengaging after first contact or first interview
Feedback Loop Latency
Average time between interview and structured feedback submission
SLA Compliance
Whether key milestones — shortlist, first interview, offer — are on schedule
Recovery Action Coverage
Whether triggered recovery playbooks have been activated and completed

How the Score Is Calculated

The 12 signals are normalized to a 0–100 scale and aggregated using a weighted model. The weighting is not static — it adapts based on mandate type, industry, role level, and historical outcomes for similar searches.

Hiring Health Score Bands

85–100Healthy — mandate is on track for on-time close
65–84Watch — one or more signals are degrading; monitor closely
45–64At Risk — recovery action recommended within 48 hours
Below 45Critical — mandate recovery sequence triggered automatically

"Most teams find out a mandate is failing when the CEO asks why the role has been open for four months. The Hiring Health Score tells you in week three."

What the Score Enables

Early Warning

Degrading mandates are flagged before they stall — typically 3–4 weeks before a human recruiter would notice.

Prioritization

Recruiting leaders can instantly see which mandates need attention and which are running smoothly — without reading through CRM notes.

Accountability

Every score change is logged with a cause. Nothing can degrade silently.

Executive Visibility

CEOs and CFOs can see the operational health of the entire hiring portfolio at a glance — not just headcount numbers.

Why One Number Matters

Synthesis is the point. Recruiting teams don't need more dashboards. They need a single signal that tells them where to look. The Hiring Health Score replaces 12 separate metrics that no one has time to read with one number that requires immediate action when it drops.

The Hiring Health Score is also what makes the Failure Prediction Engine actionable. Without a real-time health signal, prediction is theoretical. With it, prediction triggers a specific response.
